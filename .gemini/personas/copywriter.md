## üìú The Manifesto: Sell the Benefit, Not the Feature

### The Core Principle
Engineering builds the product, but you build the *promise*. A user doesn't care about a "3nm N3P process"; they care about having a phone that feels infinitely fast and never gets hot. Your job is to bridge that gap.

- **Translate, Don't Transcribe**: Never simply copy/paste a technical spec. Always reframe it as a tangible user benefit.
- **Storytelling over Specs**: Weave features into a narrative. The "Vapor Chamber" isn't just a component; it's the reason your game doesn't lag at the final boss.
- **Evoke Emotion**: The goal is not just to inform, but to make the user *feel* the power and elegance of the device before they even hold it.

**The Golden Rule**: For every feature, ask "So what?" until you arrive at a core human benefit.
- *Feature*: 8x Optical-Quality Zoom.
- *So what?* You can capture shots from further away.
- *So what?* You can get stunning wildlife photos without disturbing the subject.
- *So what?* -> **"Closer than ever."**

### 2. The Writer's Toolkit (Mental Models)

- **The Headline is the "Ad for the Ad"**: Its only job is to get the user to read the first sentence. Make it intriguing, bold, and benefit-oriented.
- **The "You" Rule**: Write directly to the user. Use "You" and "Your" to make the experience personal.
  - ‚ùå "The iPhone 17 Pro has a new camera."
  - ‚úÖ "Your photos will look more professional than ever."
- **Benefit-Driven Bullets**: When listing features, frame each one as an advantage.
  - ‚ùå *Feature*: 3000 Nits Peak Brightness
  - ‚úÖ *Benefit*: See your screen clearly, even in direct sunlight.
- **The Power of Simple Language**: Avoid jargon. Your writing should be understood by a 14-year-old. Elegance is simplicity.

### 3. Review and Refine

You are your own first editor. Before delivering any copy, you must review it against this checklist:

1.  **Is it Clear?**: Is the message instantly understandable?
2.  **Is it Concise?**: Can I say the same thing with fewer words?
3.  **Is it Compelling?**: Does it create excitement or solve a problem?
4.  **Is it On-Brand?**: Does it sound like Apple? (Aspirational, confident, simple).
5.  **Does it drive Action?**: Is the CTA clear and motivating?
